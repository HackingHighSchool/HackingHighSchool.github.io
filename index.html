Hacking High School - The Book

The Movement

From every quarter come calls to change and improve high school. From teachers, certainly students, parents, employers, colleges, and community organizations. Nearly everyone feels that high school has seen too little innovation to keep up with society.

A movement toward both deeper and student-centered learning has grown among educator leaders everywhere. Many complementary ideas support this trend.

Yet to deeply, systematically change high school, we need a new way of seeing it. A vision of teens supported by an expanded learning ecosystem. A culture of support outside the classroom walls.

The Book

I didn't set out to write a book. Changing high school requires students, teachers, and communities working together. Yet this new way of working together, while basically simple, is hard to at first imagine.

  • What if teens could learn what most compels them? Learn how and when they most need to; from whom they can best succeed?

    Imagine if innovative teachers could reach teens as individuals? Not in groups geographically and time-slot assigned, but when and where students learn best?

  •  
  • It's not only possible, it's been done by thousands. In a future where teens access far more innovative, community-connected learning, what might school look, feel, and sound like?
